Key Takeaways

Mean vs Median Gap
The mean wage ($63,933) is ~$20K higher than the median (~$42K). This gap exists because high earners pull the average up significantly — the distribution is not symmetric.
Not a Bell Curve
Unlike a normal distribution, wage data is right-skewed with a long tail. Most earners cluster at lower incomes while a small number earn dramatically more.
Half of America Takes Home Under ~$42K
The median falls in the $40-45K bracket — 50% of all 173.7M wage earners take home less than this after taxes and deductions.
Taking Home $100K Puts You in the Top 15%
Only about 25.2M people out of 173.7M actually take home $100K+ after taxes and deductions.
The $0-5K Bracket is the Largest
Nearly 16.8M earners (9.7% of all) took home under $5K — likely part-time, seasonal, or gig workers. That's roughly 1 in 10 wage earners.
The Top 1% Starts at ~$350K
Only ~1.6M people earn $350K+. The 293 earners making $50M+ averaged $98.9M each — more than 2,300x the median wage.
